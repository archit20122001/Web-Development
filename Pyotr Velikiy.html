Russain Battlecruiser Pyotr Velikiy

 Pyotr Velikiy is the last ship in the Kirov class battlecruiser, built for Soviet Navy. It currently serves as the flagship of the Northern Sea fleet of the Russian Navy. Originaly named Yuri Andrapov, the former general seceratory of the Communist Party, the ship's name was changed to Pyotr Velikiy after the fall of Soviet Union. The Russian designation for this type of ship is Tyazheliy Atomnyi Raketnyi Kreiser ,but the Western world considers them as Battlecruiser, because of their shir size and firepower.

 Construction of the ship was delayed by the national and economic problems before and after the fall of Soviet Union. It was not completed and commissioned until 1998, twelve years after work had started. by then, it had been renamed Pyotr Velikiy. Pyotr Velikiy carries a pennent number "099".

Service History

After completing its acceptence trials in 1996, the vessel was transferred to the Northern Fleet at Severomorsk and was designated as the Flagship of the Northern Fleet.


In August 2000, Pyotr Velikiy was in the Barent Sea to oversee the largest naval training exercise in a decade, since the fall of Soviet Union. The ship was designated to be the target for the Oscar II class submarine K-141 Kursk, and was conducting evasive manouvers when communication with Kursk was lost. The submarine was found to have suffered catastrophic torpedo detonations, resulting in the loss of 118 crew members. Pyotr Velikiy guarded the area where the submarine sank during the subsequent salvage operation in 2001. In March 2004, Russian Navy Chief Admiral Vladimir Kuroyedov declared Pyotr Velikiy unfit for service due to problem with ship's engineering maintainance. on 19 April 2004, the cruiser was docked in the floating drydock PD 50 for painting of the underside of the hull, repairs and examination of the steering system. The repairs were completed later that year, and it was carrying missions again by August.
From September 21 to October 22, 2004 she sailed on her first long voyage. Accompanying the aircraft carrier Admiral Kuznetsov,the destroyers Admiral Chabanenko and Admiral Ushakov, as well as several auxiliary ships, she went into the North-West Atlantic in what was the largest Russian naval exercise after the end of Cold War. During this time, Pyotr Velikiy demonstrated its various capabilities like, anti missile defence, anti air defence, anti ballistic missile defence and many more, its also used its P 700 Granits missile in the swarm technique where the missiles are launched in salvoes of 4 to 8 and they fly to the target with the lead missile designating different targets while the rest of the missiles track and destroyt their designated targets, if somehow, the lead missile gets destroyed, another missile from the group takes its position and starts target designation. On 8 September 2008,it was announced that Pyotr Velikiy would sail to Caribbean Sea to participate in naval exercise with Venezuelan Navy, along with destroyer Admiral Chabanenko and other support ships. This action would represent the first major Russian show of force in the Caribbean since the end of the Cold War. On 22 September, Pyotr Velikiy and Admiral Chabanenko left their home port of Severomorsk.
On 22 October 2008 Pyotr Velikiy made a port visit to Aksaz Karagac, Turkey and on 6-9 November the cruiser and Admiral Chabanenko made a port visit to Toulon, France, before departing the Medieterranean on 10 November, passing through the Strait of Gibraltar.
Pyotr Velikiy arrived in La Guaira, Venezuela on 25 November 2008 coinciding the visit by Russian President Dimtri Medvedev. A combined exercise, VENRUS-200, with the Venezuelan Navy took place on 1-2 December 2008. After finishing the exercises, Admiral Chabanenko made a short visit to Panama 5-10 December, then to Bluefields, Nicaragua from 13 to 15 December and sailed into Havana, Cuba on 19 December.
Pyotr Velikiy continued alone to Cape Town, South Africa. On 11 January 2009, the chief of Russian staff announced that Pyotr Velikiy and six other warships would participate in a joint naval exercise with Indian Navy later the same month. The Kirov-class cruiser paused for three days to visit Cape Town before continuing on to India.


On 31 January Pyotr Velikiy left the port of Mormugao in the Indian state of Goa. After a two-day visit that included a navl exercise with Indian guided missile destroyer INS Delhi the cruiser left for African waters where the vessels joined other warships from the Russian navy and conduct the INDRA-2009 exercise.
On 12 February, the ship captured ten pirates in three boats off the coast of Somalia.
On 10 March Pyotr Velikiy returned to its homeport of Severomorsk, ending a six-month deployment.
On 30 March 2010 Pyotr Velikiy left the Northern Fleet for a new six-month deployment. During its six-month tour of duty, the warship passed through Atlantic Ocean and the Indian Ocean via Suez Canal. In the Indian Ocean the cruiser conducted maneuvers with other Russian warships of the Black Sea Fleet.
On 14 April, the missile cruiser visited the Mediterranean port of Tartus in Syria. In September 2008, Russia was reported to be in talks with Syria about turning Tartus into a permanent base for Russian warships in the Middle East.


In early May 2010, Pyotr Velikiy met with cruiser Moskva in the South China Sea. There they conducted joint exercises and held a traditional farewell ceremony on 5 May. The two vessels were due to arrive in Russia's Far Eastern port of Vladivostok to take part in VOSTOK-2010 large scale strategic exercise.
On 29 September Pyotr Velikiy returned to its home base in the Northern Fleet after six months at sea. The flagship of the Northhern Fleet had covered 28,000 nautical miles(52,000 km; 32,000 mi) since the beginning of the mission on 30 March.
On 20 October, Pyotr Velikiy departed from the base of Severomorsk to pay a visit to the Bukhta Abrek bay in the Pacific region to meet her sister ship, Admiral Lazarev, which is anchored at the bay waiting to be scrapped after its decommisioning, thereafter it continued to the naval base of Vladivostok, where the ship conducted exercises and maneouvers with the Black Sea Fleet and them returned to port on 30 December.
Pyotr Velikiy, along with aircraft carrier Admiral Kuznetsov and the tanker Sergey Osipov, Kama and Dubna; the tugboat Atley, and the Repucha-class landing ship, Minsk(122) entered the English Channel to sail north. The British destroyer HMS Dragon monitered the Russian task force as it neared the United Kingdom. Once, the ship spotted each other they sailed briefly close by as a standard 'meet and greet'.
In May 2016 Pyotr Velikiy left Severomorsk to escort Admiral Kuznetsov to the Mediterrenean along with supply ships and two Udaloy-class destroyer, Severomorsk and Vice-Admiral Kulakov. They were heading to the Eastern Mediterrenean to to support Syrian government forces battleing the rebel troops in Aleppo. Pyotr Velikiy passed through the English Channel along with the rest of the Russian Northern Fleet on 21 October, shadowed by the British destroyer HMS Dragon.


On 6 April 2019, the ships entered Barent Sea with cruiser Marshal Ustinov and Several nuclear submarines.
On 10 October 2019 she went into Barent Sea with some 15 other warships, submarines and other auxilary ships to conduct large-scale drills.
On 24 May 2021 Pyotr Velikiy went to sea along with some 10 other warships, including Marshal Ustinov. On 7 June, Pyotr Velikiy was still reported at sea taking part in 20 ship strong exercise. On 1 July 2021, it went to sea again.
On 15 September 2021, the cruiser conducted an exercise in the Barents Sea along with the cruiser Marshal Ustinov, both shooting Granit and Bulkan missiles, respectively.
On 29 January 2022, the cruiser conducted an air defence drill, without leaving the port.
On 15 February, Pyotr Veliky, frigate Admiral Gorshkov, diesel and nuclear submarines started an exercise in the Barents Sea.
Between 15 and 17 March 2022, she and destroyer Severomorsk held an exercise between Norway and Iceland, during the large-scale NATO naval exercise.
On 18 April 2022, Pyotr Veliky was at sea again, conducting an artillery exercise in the Barents Sea.
In June, the sailors started preparation for the Navy day parade in July.
Between 17 and 26 August 2022, Pyotr Veliky was at sea during a large-scale exercise and shot a Granit missile on 24 August. She was accompanied by destroyer Admiral Ushakov and an unknown number of submarines. At the same time, in the eastern part of the Barents Sea, destroyer Admiral Levchenko, LST Aleksandr Otrakovsky and tanker Sergey Osipov were active and embarked on a voyage eastwards along the Northern Sea Route. During the exercise, destroyer Severomorsk and LST Ivan Gren also passed through the Barents Sea on a way from the main naval parade in St. Petersburg to Severomorsk.
